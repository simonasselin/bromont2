SECURITY


We recommend that you never venture out alone on the mountain. If you do, it would be wise to bring a cell phone along.

Make sure you always ride on open, marked trails. As well as avoiding unnecessary risks, you'll also help prevent erosion on the mountain and potential damage to the environment.

The mountain has 51 numbered markers called "emergency points." These serve as reference points for patrollers to facilitate their operations. If you need help on the mountain, find the closest marker and dial 450 534-2200, ext. 2842. Our patrollers will then be able to quickly locate your position on the trail.

 

If you must stop during your descent, get off the trail as quickly as possible – for your own safety as well as the safety of other riders. Reduce the risk of accidents or annoyance by respecting other riders and keeping a reasonable distance between you and the person in front.

To keep your personal items safe at Ski Bromont, you can rent a locker for the day at a cost of $5 for a small locker* and $10 for a larger one**. Keys can be picked up at the Customer Service Department.

*You get $2 back when you return the key the same day.

**You get $5 back when you return the key the same day.

EQUIPMENT AND CLOTHING


Carrying a bulky wallet can be a nuisance when you're practicing a demanding sport. You can avoid this by purchasing a gift card from our Customer Service Department or online. Choose the value that's right for you and use the card to make purchases in our shop, restaurant and in most retail outlets throughout the resort. It's simple, handy and safer.

Plan a budget for little extras. You might need something practical, or feel like a dip at the Waterpark, or a beer at the end of the day. Make your day at Ski Bromont as good as it can be!

Make sure you dress properly in long, resistant clothes. If you need advice, don't hesitate to ask the staff in our shop. They will be glad to assist you.

Wearing a helmet is mandatory everywhere on the trail network. We also recommend closed shoes, gloves, knee and elbow guards and, for the more adventurous ones, protective eyewear.

It's important to choose a helmet that fits YOU properly (whatever the model) as well as a good pair of cushioned gloves. If you're not sure, ask the staff in our shop. They will help you match the right accessories to your needs.
